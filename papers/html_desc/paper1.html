The Density-Feedback Faddeev–Niemi Hopfion: Exact Algebraic Predictions for Standard-Model Parameters

We study a density-feedback modification of the Faddeev–Niemi Hopfion functional and show that its self-consistent saddle-point solution carries a rich algebraic structure rooted in the McKay correspondence for the binary icosahedral group 2I. The vacuum is proved unique: the Q = 2 icosahedral Hopfion is the minimum-action configuration at the condensate scale Λcond = TCMB(π2/150)1/4.

Three exact algebraic identities follow from φ2 = φ + 1 alone, without free parameters: the feedback stiffness Kfb = 2φ J2a, the scale-selection equivalence sopt = 21/6 ⇔ J4/J2a = 24/3/φ5, and the topological charge ord(q2I) = 10. The scale-selection value sopt = 21/6 is confirmed numerically to 0.010% and proved analytically across Papers II and III.

Conditional on the 2I/E8 group-theoretic input, the following Standard-Model observables are predicted with no fitted parameters:

  • Weinberg angle (unconditional) sin2θW = 3/(8φ) ≈ 0.2318, deviation 0.23% from the PDG value. Proved via the Hopf submersion amplitude ratio AY/AT3 = 1/φ.
  • Electron Yukawa ye = exp(−25π/6) ≈ 2.066×10−6, giving vEW ≈ 247.4 GeV (0.46% from 246.2 GeV).
  • Koide ratio K = 2/3 exactly, from Z3 Fourier symmetry of the WZW T-matrix phases.
  • Koide angle θK = 132.550°, deviation 0.14% from 132.732° (measured lepton masses).
  • Lepton mass ratios mμ/me = 206.69 (0.04% from 206.77) and mτ/me = 3476.2 (0.03% from 3477.2).
  • W/Z mass ratio MW/MZ = √(1−3/(8φ)) ≈ 0.8765, deviation 0.56%.
The two experimental inputs: TCMB = 2.7255 K and me = 0.511 MeV, in prior versions of this paper have been reduced to a single experimental input: TCMB = 2.7255 K. Paper XII proves the profile normalisation conjecture φ9√(J2aJ4) = 10 via the CMB thermodynamic equilibrium condition, completing the one-parameter chain TCMB → ρCMB = 10 → φ9√(J2aJ4) = 0 → me = Λcond φ20 e4π−3/400 with no second input. The golden ratio φ enters through two independent proved routes: as the quantum dimension dimq(1/2; k = 3) of the SU(2)3 WZW model, and as the character χΓ2(C5) of the fundamental 2I representation. The Bogomolny parameter λ = φ6 is the unique value consistent with Qnum = Qgroup = 10.
