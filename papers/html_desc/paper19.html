Paper XVI documented a systematic negative result: six candidate mechanisms for the quark generation mass hierarchy each fail for a specific structural reason, and identified two patterns — no mechanism alone supplies generation-dependent input, and a dynamical correction beyond any representation-theoretic backbone is required. Paper XIX confirms and refines this picture. Two facts established in the companion draft Paper XVIII change what the calculation is: the ℤ3 symmetry of the ideal trefoil T(2,3) is broken from the outset by the single perturbation site of the formation event, and the QH=3 configuration is transient and unstable, so its observables are properties of the formation-and-decay process rather than of a static geometry. Quark masses are therefore treated not as intrinsic energy scales of the trefoil, but as characteristic scales of how a single incoming perturbation partitions across the trefoil geometry during transit.
