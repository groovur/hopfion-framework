The Profile Normalisation Conjecture: Proof via CMB Energy Identity, Icosahedral Newton Series, and Two-Route Equivalence

We prove the profile normalisation conjecture φ9√(J2aJ4) = 10 of the density-feedback Faddeev–Niemi Hopfion series, closing the last open step in the derivation of the electron mass from the CMB temperature alone. The proof is a pure algebraic equivalence requiring no numerical input: the conjecture is shown to be equivalent to the thermodynamic equilibrium condition Efb / Vtorus = ρCMB, both conditions reducing to the same geometric constraint R0C*2 = 5 / (φ23π2) on the condensate torus.

Five results are established. Theorem A (CMB energy identity): The condensate scale Λcond = TCMB(π2/150)1/4 satisfies ρCMB = 10Λcond4 exactly (150/15 = 10, a pure arithmetic identity), where 10 = ord(q2I) = 2(k+2)|k=3 is the icosahedral group-order invariant. Theorem B (Pentagon identities): φ = 2cos(π/5) and with μ* = 3 − φ: sin(π/5) = √μ*/2, sin(2π/5) = φ√μ*/2. These are the trigonometric expressions of the Pentagon Theorem (k+2 = 5), and they drive the Newton series of Theorem C. Theorem C (Newton–icosahedral series): The virial fixed point b* satisfying (15/8) arctan(√(8b*)) / √(8b*) = φ has the quadratically-convergent icosahedral Newton series q* = π/5 + d1 + d2 + … with b* = tan2(q*)/8, first correction d1 = (φ√μ*/12) · (8√μ* − 3π) / (5φ√μ*/4 − π) ≈ +4.19×10−3, and subsequent terms satisfying |dn+1| = O(|dn|2).
