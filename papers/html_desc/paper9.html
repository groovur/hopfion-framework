We identify the algebraic structure underlying the Bell correlations of Paper VIII. The condensate suppression weight sin4 θ/φ6 from the prior papers is precisely the quaternionic (ℍ-valued) norm on transition amplitudes: the factor sin4 θ = |sin2 θ|2 is the squared modulus of a quaternion-valued amplitude, contrasting with the complex norm |ψ|2 of standard quantum mechanics. The condensate correlation function xcond(θ) = 2 cos θ/(1 + cos2θ) is identified as the push-forward of the standard quantum correlation xQM(θ) = cos θ under the Hopf fibration S3 → S2, the natural map from quaternionic to complex amplitudes, with the angle relation tan(θℍ/2) = tan2(θℂ/2).

Three foundational results are proved in the first part of the paper. Theorem (Hopf push-forward): xcond is the push-forward of xQM under the Hopf fibration, with xcond = xQM / (1 − ⅓(1 − xQM2)) from the Jacobian of S3 → S2. Theorem (Born rule from complexification): The Born rule P = |ψ|2 emerges from the condensate suppression weights when amplitudes are complexified via the topological winding phase Φ = Q · ω, where Q = 10 is the Hopf charge and ω is the solid angle subtended at mismatch angle θ; the quantisation of Q ∈ π3(S2) = ℤ is proved in companion Paper X. Theorem (Tsirelson gap decomposition): The raw CHSH of the condensate is CHSHraw ≈ 0.886; after rescaling by the planar geometric factor 8/3 this gives CHSHrescaled ≈ 2.363, leaving a gap of ≈ 0.47 below Tsirelson’s bound 2√2 ≈ 2.828. The gap decomposes exactly into a Fourier harmonic mismatch between xcond and xQM, and a geometric projection error from the ℍ → ℂ averaging; both vanish under the pointwise ℍ → ℂ projection. The exact Fourier coefficients are A2k+1 = (4 − 2√2) · (√2 − 3)k, with Parseval sum ∑An2/2 = 1/√2 (exact).

Three further results extend the framework to measurement strength and density feedback. Theorem (CHSH(f) transition): A one-parameter family of condensate probabilities pf(θ) interpolates from the standard Born rule (f = 1, CHSHrescaled = 1.742) through the quaternionic condensate (f = 2, CHSHrescaled ≈ 2.363) to the projective limit (f → ∞, ceiling 8/3 ≈ 2.667 for leptons); the rescaled CHSH is strictly monotone in f and the ℍ → ℂ projection recovers Tsirelson’s bound at each f. Theorem (density feedback): Incorporating the density-feedback coupling β* = 0.452 from the Bogomolny self-consistency condition gives CHSHrescaled ≈ 2.341 at f = 2 and ≈ 2.60 at f = 4, in agreement with the Monte Carlo result of Paper VIII.

Four results derive the photon coupling from first principles. Theorem (interaction Hamiltonian): The condensate–photon interaction Hamiltonian Hint = e JμAμ follows from the shift-symmetry Noether current of the k-essence condensate action P(X) = X/[φ6(1 + β*X)], giving matrix element ⟨φn|Hint|α⟩ = geff cos(φn − α), with effective coupling geff = 2eA0|∇ρ0|/[φ6(1 + β*X0)2]. The same factor φ6 suppresses both the Bell correlation law sin4 θ/φ6 and the photon coupling — a microscopic unity of Bell and Malus phenomena arising from the single condensate action. Theorem (Malus’s law): Normalising the interaction amplitude over two polarization outcomes gives the transmission probability T(φn,α) = cos2(φn − α); Malus’s law is a theorem, not an assumption. Theorem (photon winding number): The photon polarization observable in the condensate is uniquely the m = 2 Fourier mode of S1, determined by two constraints: the Malus transmission probability and the π-periodicity of polarization (photon polarization lives on ℝP1 = S1/ℤ2, giving a 2:1 covering of the condensate circle). Theorem (Tsirelson exact): The Stokes/Malus observable cos(2(φn − α)) gives CHSH = 2√2 = Tsirelson exactly; the Parseval factor ⅓ for the m = 2 mode on L2(S1) provides the geometric explanation of the QM normalisation convention, and is now derived from the condensate action.

The condensate model predicts two distinct CHSH ceilings depending on the observable and particle species. For leptons measured with the quaternionic pcond observable xcond(θ), the projective ceiling is 8/3 ≈ 2.667 (a structural prediction for loophole-free lepton Bell tests). For photons measured with the Stokes/Malus observable, the ceiling is exactly 2√2 = Tsirelson; all existing loophole-free photon Bell experiments — including Poh et al. (2015) who reached S = 2.82759 ± 0.00051, within 0.03% of Tsirelson — are fully consistent with the condensate prediction. The structural gap 2√2 − 8/3 ≈ 0.161 is a falsifiable prediction for lepton Bell tests where the ℍ-valued observable applies. A post-quantum conjecture predicts CHSH = 8√2/3 ≈ 3.771 if a future experiment couples to the condensate via the pcond (quaternionic) weight rather than Malus transmission for a spin-1 particle; this exceeds Tsirelson’s quantum bound while satisfying no-signaling.
